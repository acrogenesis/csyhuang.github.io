I have just completed my Ph.D degree in Geophysical Sciences in the University of Chicago and currently work as a postdoctoral research assistant in Professor Noboru Nakamura's group. My dissertation focuses on developing an atmospheric Rossby wave diagnostic theory that quantifies severity of extreme weather events. My current work focuses on the search of predictive features of atmospheric blocking. Check out my python library to implement this diagnosis on climate data!

I also participated in the Insight Data Science Fellowship Program in 2017 summer as a Health Data Fellow to learn about data science and health industry. I worked on a consulting project for an online birth control pill selling platform on churn prediction. You can find my mini-data-projects and technical learning journals here.
